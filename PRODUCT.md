# CaterFlow — Catering Operations Platform

## Brand
- **Product name**: CaterFlow
- **Category**: Catering Operations Platform
- **Tagline**: Every event. Every detail. Under control.
- **Promise**: From scattered chaos to calm, organised growth.
- **Tone**: Professional, calm, purposeful. Speaks to operators who take quality seriously. Avoids hype; uses concrete, outcome-oriented language.

## Users
| Persona | Role |
|---|---|
| **Catering Owner** | Primary user. Runs multiple simultaneous events; needs command-centre visibility to maintain quality at scale. |
| **Event Coordinator** | Secondary user. Executes day-of logistics; relies on accurate staff rosters, menus, and vendor schedules. |

## Problem
Catering owners juggling multiple events lean on spreadsheets, sticky notes, and group chats. The result: missed details, double-booked staff, mismatched menus — and quality that suffers precisely when it matters most.

## Solution
One calm, purpose-built workspace that connects every event order, menu, and staff assignment — delivering complete visibility across all events simultaneously.

## Core Features

### 1. Event Order Management
Create and track every event order — client, date, venue, headcount, budget, and live status — on a master calendar dashboard.
- Tags: Orders · Calendar · Status · Confirmed · Prep · Day-of · Completed

### 2. Menu Planning
Build reusable menus and assign dishes to events. Flag dietary restrictions and allergens so every guest's needs are accounted for.
- Tags: Menus · Dishes · Allergens · Dietary Restrictions · Reusable Templates

### 3. Staff Assignments
Assign staff roles to each event, check availability conflicts in real time, and see at a glance who is allocated where across all events.
- Tags: Roles · Availability · Conflicts · Real-time Conflict Detection

### 4. Multi-Event Dashboard
A command-centre overview with status indicators — Confirmed, Prep, Day-of, Completed — across every active event so nothing falls through the cracks.
- Tags: Overview · Status · Control · Command Centre

### 5. Vendor Management
Organise every supplier into categories (Florist, Linen & Decor, AV & Equipment, Catering Supplies, Transport, and more). Store full vendor info (name, contact, company, notes), assign required roles per event, and set work time frames (lead time, setup window, on-site hours) so every vendor is perfectly scheduled and nothing overlaps.
- Tags: Categories · Vendor Info · Required Roles · Time Frames · Scheduling

## Value Propositions
| Pillar | Headline | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Visibility** | Nothing slips | Every order, menu, and staff assignment lives in one place — no more scattered notes or details lost across events. |
| **Staffing** | Zero double-bookings | Built-in conflict detection flags scheduling clashes before they cause problems. |
| **Quality** | Consistent standards | Reusable menus and standardised workflows keep service quality high across one event or ten. |
| **Scalability** | Handle more, stress less | A command-centre dashboard keeps every active event in view so owners can confidently take on more business. |

## Strategic Principles
1. **Single workspace** — all event data lives in one place; no integrations required for the core loop.
2. **Conflict prevention over correction** — surfaces double-bookings and scheduling overlaps before they happen.
3. **Reusability** — menus, staff rosters, and vendor lists are assets that compound across events.
4. **Scalability without chaos** — the product should make it easier to take on more events, not just manage fewer better.
